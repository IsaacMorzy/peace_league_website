Ceremony Schedule

December 17, 2026

A day of honour, remembrance, and celebration — from registration through to the closing address.

08:00 – 09:00

Arrival, Registration & Welcome Coffee

Guests arrive, register at the welcome desk, and enjoy coffee and light refreshments. Name badges and programmes distributed.

09

09:00 – 10:00

Pre-Ceremony Gathering & Exhibition

Networking session with exhibition stands showcasing peacebuilding initiatives from across 12 African countries. Meet the nominees and learn about their work.

10

10:00 – 10:15

Opening Ceremony

National Anthem. Welcome Address by Dr. James Nderitu, Executive Director of Peace League Africa. Opening remarks on the significance of the venue and the awards.

🎤

10:15 – 10:45

Keynote Address

H.E. Fatuma Mohamed, Cabinet Secretary, Ministry of Interior & Coordination — Kenya. A powerful address on the state of peacebuilding in Africa and the critical role of community-led initiatives.

11

10:45 – 11:30

Award Session 1: Peace Advocacy & Humanitarian Service

Presentation of awards recognising individuals and organizations that have demonstrated exceptional commitment to peace advocacy, conflict resolution, and humanitarian service across Africa.

🎵

11:30 – 11:45

Cultural Interlude

A vibrant showcase of African music, dance, and spoken word — celebrating the rich cultural heritage of the communities we serve. Featuring artists from Kenya, Uganda, and Rwanda.

12

11:45 – 12:30

Award Session 2: Youth Leadership & Community Development

Honouring young leaders and community organisers who are driving change from the ground up. Categories include youth peace ambassadors, community development champions, and grassroots innovators.

12:30 – 13:30

Lunch Break & Networking

A catered lunch with opportunities to network with fellow peacebuilders, nominees, and distinguished guests. Dietary requirements accommodated (halal, vegetarian, gluten-free).

🏆

13:30 – 14:15

Award Session 3: Special Recognitions & Lifetime Achievement

The most anticipated session of the day. Special recognitions for extraordinary contributions, including the Lifetime Achievement Award honouring a peacebuilder whose work has transformed communities across generations.

14:15 – 14:45

Closing Remarks & Call to Action

A unifying address from Amb. Olusegun Akinlade, African Union Special Envoy for Conflict Resolution. A call to action for all attendees to continue building peace in their communities.

14:45 – 15:00

Group Photo & Media Interviews

All award winners, nominees, and distinguished guests gather for the official group photograph. Media interviews with winners. Event concludes.

The Venue

August 7th Memorial Park

A place of remembrance, resilience, and the enduring power of peace — chosen deliberately as the setting for this historic ceremony.

Location

Junction of Haile Selassie Avenue & Moi Avenue, Nairobi City Centre (next to Cooperative Bank)

Why This Venue

The August 7th Memorial Park stands on the site of the 1998 US Embassy bombing, a tragedy that claimed 218 lives. By hosting our awards here, we honour those lost while celebrating the peacebuilders who choose hope over hate, dialogue over division.

Park Features

The Memorial Wall with 218 names, indigenous Kenyan plants, the Yin-Yang fountain, the 'Mind, Body & Spirit' sculpture from original debris, and riverbed rock walkways — every element tells a story of resilience.
